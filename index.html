Simhachalam Darsan

The Varaha Lakshmi Narasimha temple is a South Indian Hindu temple situated on the Simhachalam hill, which is 800 metres above the sea level at a distance of ten miles to the north of Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh. It is said that lord Vishnu manifested in this peculiar form, with a boar head, human torso, and a lion's tail, after saving his devotee Prahlada from a murder attempt by the latter's father Hiranyakashipu. This temple was originally concerted by the greatest of the devotees, the Prahlada, and later it underwent many radical changes. Though only the outer temple architecture underwent many changes, the inner energy vortex of the temple is intact and is considered highly aggressive in nature. Siddha Guru Atmananda Ji gracefully accepted our request to take the sadhaks of Supramental Healer’s batch and guide us not only in understanding the temple and it’s various necities, but also has promised to make us experience and learn the secrets experiences of the energy vortex manifested there. Importance of this program


Vishnu Sahasranama Nigooda Rahasya is the most powerful and the easiest way for wholistic abundance. The holy mother, Tripuraamba, is a combination of SIVA and SHAKTHI and is the bestower of Bhukthi (material bliss) and Mukthi (Spritual Bliss). But the holy mother, who is also known as “Karanguli nakhothpanna narayana dasakrithi”, is the bestower of all material gains known to man when worshiped after understanding the secrets of the Vishnu Sahasranama. The mother is also called “Narayani” because the Vishnu is masculine form of the Adi Para Shakthi.The Vishnu Sahasranaama was composed by Sri Veda Vyaasa, the author of the Puraanas, and we meet this great chant in his classical work, the Mahaabaarata. Prince Yudhisthira, the eldest of the Pandavas, at the end of the war, approached Bheeshma Pitaamaha, awaiting the sacred hour of his departure to the feet of the lord. Yudhishthira, the righteous, asked six questions to the great Bheeshma, who is the knower of the eternal truth. The questions being:

  1. Who ("kim") is the greatest ("ekam") Lord ("daivatam") in the world ("loke")?
  2. Who is the one ("ekam") refuge ("paraayanam") for all?
  3. By glorifying ("sthuvantah") whom ("kam") can man ("manavah") reach the Auspiciousness ("shubam") (peace and prosperity)?
  4. By worshipping ("archantah") whom can a man reach auspiciousness (peace and prosperity)?
  5. What ("ko") is, in thy opinion, the Greatest Dharma?
  6. By ("kim") doing japa of what can “creatures” (jantu) go beyond ("mutchyate") the bonds ("bandhanaath") of samsara?

The greatest among warriors and the finest among JevanMuktas ( one who has gained and assimilated self-knowledge and thus is liberated with an inner sense of freedom while living), then gives out the ancient most coded secret of the Sanathana Dharma, Vishnu Sahasranama, which is the most essential to live a happy, abundant, blissful, and righteous life. In the Sahasranama (1000 names), every name has a secret Bija Akshara and a secret experience to it. Mere chanting of the Sahasranamas is not sufficient to attain the full benefit of it. Every name in the Sahasranama can give us inexplicable bliss and blessings, when the secret Bija Aksharas are unlocked. If unlocked, the Vishnu Sahasranama is such a powerful experience that mere thinking of the name is enough for a person to go into a very deep state of meditative bliss. So, in a meditative state, understanding or experiencing the experience behind these names is Vishnu Sahasranama Nigooda Rahasya.
